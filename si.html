Samal Island

Samal Island, officially known as the Island Garden City of Samal (IGaCoS), is a premier tropical destination in the Davao Gulf, Mindanao. It is the largest resort city in the Philippines, home to over 30 beach resorts ranging from budget-friendly hostels to five-star luxury villas.

Key Highlights & Attractions

  • Pristine Beaches: Known for its powdery white sand and turquoise waters, popular spots include Kaputian Beach, Canibad Beach, and the luxurious Pearl Farm Beach Resort.
  • Natural Wonders: Monfort Bat Sanctuary: Holds the Guinness World Record for the largest documented colony of fruit bats. Hagimit Falls: A series of cascading natural waterfalls and pools surrounded by lush greenery. Giant Clam Sanctuary: A protected area where visitors can snorkel to see thousands of giant clams.
  • Island Hopping: Talikud Island, located just southwest, is a favorite for snorkeling and diving due to its vibrant coral gardens.
  • Adventure: Vanishing Island is a sandbar that disappears during high tide, while Mt. Puting Bato offers trekking with panoramic views of the gulf.

Essential Travel Info (2026)

  • How to Get There: Fly to Davao International Airport, then take a 15–20 minute ferry ride from the Sasa Wharf or Santa Ana Wharf in Davao City.
  • Best Time to Visit: The dry season from December to May offers the best weather for island activities.
