Talamo Beach

Talomo Beach in Davao City is a historically significant coastal area known for its unique black volcanic sand and proximity to the city center. While it was a premier destination in the 1970s and 80s, parts of the public shoreline now face challenges with cleanliness and steep, "trench-y" underwater slopes.

Most visitors today experience the beach through established resorts that offer better-maintained facilities and swimming pools.

Popular Beach Resorts in Talomo

  • Mergrande Ocean Resort: A well-known spot in Dumoy featuring a swimming pool, playground, and cottages. It is favored for family reunions and has a gradual shoreline.
  • Waterworld Beach Resort: Located in Dumoy, this resort is popular for its multiple pools and water slides.
  • Times Beach: Once a famous swimming spot, it has largely transitioned into a "food street" known for affordable fresh seafood restaurants like Liza's.

Historical Significance

Talomo Beach holds deep historical roots as a strategic naval beachhead used by Japanese, American, and Spanish forces. During the Japanese occupation, the area was known as "Little Tokyo" due to the dense population of Japanese families living along the shoreline.

Visitor Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: The dry season from January to May offers the best weather for outdoor beach activities.
  • Safety: Be cautious when swimming in public areas; the sea can get deep very quickly, and waves can be strong, especially for children.
  • Entrance: While public access to the shoreline is generally free, resorts typically charge an entrance fee (e.g., around ₱100–₱190).
